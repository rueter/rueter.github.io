[[Modern Saami languages|Saami languages]] are members of the Uralic (Finno-Ugric) language family. Typical to the most of the Saami languages are the following linguistic features: </P> <ul> <li>the languages are suffixing (basicly agglutinative) languages (use endings to mark grammatical relations) but to a high degree also fusional due to the phonetic and morphophonetic development in [[Proto-Saami|Proto-Saami]] <li>they have no grammatical gender <li>there are no definite or indefinite articles <li>in grammatical categories related to persons (personal pronouns, personal endings in verbs, possessive suffixes in nouns) there are three numbers: singular, dual and plural <li>negation in verbs is expressed with the help of a negative auxiliary and connegative form of the main verb </ul> <P align="justify"> North Saami has appr. 30 000 native speakers. The neighbouring Saami languages are [[Lule Saami language|Lule Saami]] in the west and [[Inari Saami language|Inari]] and [[Skolt Saami language|Skolt Saami]] in the east, in the municipality of Inari, where all these three Saami languages are spoken. North Saami can be divided to several dialects. The main dialects are the Finnmark Saami (divided further into eastern and western subdialects), Sea Saami (on the Northern coast of Norway) and Torne Saami. The dialects differ to a great degree especially in their phonetic structure. </P> <P align="justify"> Grammatical relations in nouns are expressed by case endings (case forms).
